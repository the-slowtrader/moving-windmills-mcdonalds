Bridge the gap with us

We charge 200 Malawian kwacha — about 10 cents — for every plate we serve. The real cost is closer to $1 when you count ingredients, cooking, and distribution across 21 churches, mosques, and 4 school hubs.

That 90-cent difference? That's the gap. And you can help us close it.

Where this started

You might know the story — a fourteen-year-old in Malawi built a windmill from scrap parts and a library book. That was William Kamkwamba. He came home and founded Moving Windmills Project to invest in the community that raised him.

Today the project runs community hubs across a 40-kilometer radius in Kasungu, where people learn, build, farm — and share meals together every day.
