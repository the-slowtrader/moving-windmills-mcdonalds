“Talent and innovation are everywhere; the only thing missing is the opportunity.”
— William Kamkwamba

In 2001, fourteen-year-old William Kamkwamba built a windmill from scrap parts and a library book, bringing electricity to his family's home in Wimbe, Malawi. His story became a TED talk, a bestselling book, and a Netflix film. Then he came home and started building something bigger.

As featured in

TED Netflix Penguin Random House HarperCollins

From a library book to a movement

William returned to Wimbe — the same community where he built the windmill — and founded Moving Windmills Project. Today it runs a network of community hubs across a 40-kilometer radius, where young people learn engineering, solar technology, sustainable farming, and how to solve problems where they live.

They share meals. They teach each other. People just show up.

What's happening at the hubs

Across five locations in the Kasungu district, young people are learning to do what William did — see a problem, find a way, build something. And every day, they share a meal together.

Engineering & Innovation

Hands-on training in mechanical and electrical engineering, using locally available materials.

Solar Technology

Installing, maintaining, and designing solar systems for homes and community buildings.

Sustainable Agriculture

Climate-smart farming techniques that improve food security and protect the land.

Community Meals

Over 80,000 meals served across 21 churches and mosques and 4 school hubs — because showing up to learn is easier when no one is hungry.

The same spark, in your child's hands

We are putting that exact same spark of possibility into the hands of millions of children worldwide. A child with a dream becomes an adult with a solution.

Through SPARK, William's story becomes a classroom experience — connecting your child's curiosity to real problem-solving happening right now in Malawi.

How we got here

01

A windmill from scrap

At fourteen, William built a windmill that powered four lights and two radios. His family's home had electricity for the first time.

02

The story traveled

A TED talk. A bestselling book. A Netflix film directed by Chiwetel Ejiofor. William studied in the U.S. and at African Leadership Academy.

03

He came back

William returned to Wimbe and founded Moving Windmills Project to invest in the community that raised him.

04

Building something bigger

Today, a network of community hubs trains young people across a 40-kilometer radius in engineering, solar, and sustainable farming.
